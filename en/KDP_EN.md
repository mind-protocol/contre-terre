## 1. AMAZON DESCRIPTION

```html
<b>Seven people descend into a volcano. None of them come back up.</b><br><br>

In a world where the ground has never stopped shaking, humans invented Contact — a language that only passes through touch. Arm-locks, shoulder pressures, coded grips: when the earth's roar drowns out all speech, the body becomes the only medium.<br><br>

<b>The mission</b><br><br>

A magnitude 11 earthquake is coming. Uncontrollable. Terminal. The Consortium of Archipelagos sends seven people to the bottom of a volcano to pre-trigger the catastrophe — to cause it before it destroys them. It's a suicide mission. Everyone knows it. Seven people said yes.<br><br>

Senzo, the expedition leader. Nandi, the predictor who vomits at frequency changes and reads the ground barefoot. Sihle, the seismo-auditor who trusts only his instruments. Enama, the biologist who listens to the earth with her hands. Thabo, the air-master who tests the wind with a blackened cloth. Inyoni, the climber who counts her straps like a prayer. Jabu, the silent caver who knows the underground rivers.<br><br>

<b>The descent</b><br><br>

They die one by one. Landslide, fall, drowning, lava, collapse. Each death is geological. Each death is also linguistic: the person who disappears takes a vocabulary with them — words, gestures, nuances that only they mastered. The survivors lose the capacity for speech with every death. The language shrinks with the group.<br><br>

When only one person remains, there is no one left to speak Contact to.<br><br>

<b>Each death amputates a word.</b><br><br>

<b>Counter-Earth</b> is a novel of descent — geographic, linguistic, human. Eight chapters that are eight geological layers traversed. A physical prose where everything passes through temperature, pressure, vibration, pain. No omniscient narrator. No exposition. The world is learned through the body.<br><br>

A mirror of Alain Damasio's <i>The Horde of Counterwind</i> — where the Horde marched against the wind horizontally, this expedition descends against the earth vertically. Wind becomes rock. Surface becomes depth. Breath becomes touch.<br><br>

For readers of Damasio, Cormac McCarthy, Ted Chiang, and Jeff VanderMeer. For those seeking sensory SF with no concessions, where the invented language is not a gimmick but the beating heart of the story.
```

## 2. BACK COVER

The ground has never stopped shaking.

In this world, humans invented Contact — a fully tactile language, made of arm-locks, shoulder pressures, coded grips. The body became the only medium.

Seven people descend into a volcano. Their mission: pre-trigger a magnitude 11 earthquake before it destroys everything. No one comes back up.

They die one by one. Each death takes a vocabulary with it. Words no one will speak again. Gestures no hand will ever form.

**Each death amputates a word.**

When only one survivor remains, the language dies with her — and the mission truly begins.

*Counter-Earth* is a geological novel: eight chapters, eight layers of earth, an irreversible descent toward the center. A prose that reads with the body.
