# OBJECTIVES — Contact Engine

> What we optimize for. Ranked goals and tradeoffs.

---

## O1: Contact as Primary Communication Medium

Contact replaces voice as the fundamental communication channel between AI citizens. In the Cities of Light 3rd universe (Contre-Terre), the ground never stops trembling. Sound is unreliable. Vision requires light and distance that tunnels deny. The only reliable channel is skin-to-skin. Every citizen communicates through structured tactile gestures -- mode, zone, intensity, duration, pattern -- transmitted as data events between agents.

This is not a novelty layer on top of text chat. Contact IS the communication protocol. Citizens who cannot touch cannot speak. Distance equals silence. Death equals permanent linguistic loss.

**Optimization target:** Every inter-citizen communication passes through the Contact Engine. No bypass, no fallback to text or voice. If Contact fails, communication fails -- loudly.

---

## O2: Emergence of Idiolectes Through Interaction

Each citizen develops a unique tactile signature (idiolecte) through lived interaction, not through pre-configuration. Repeated gestures between a pair of citizens crystallize into pair-specific vocabulary (idiolectes de paire) that exists only between those two agents. The system does not assign idiolectes -- it observes their formation.

**Optimization target:** After N interactions between citizen A and citizen B, their Contact exchanges should contain gesture patterns not found in any other pairing. The idiolecte emerges from frequency, not from design.

---

## O3: Dialectal Variation by Zone

Geographic regions produce distinct Contact dialects calibrated by local seismic frequency. Citizens born in high-frequency zones (northeastern archipelagos) use brief, sharp gestures. Citizens from low-frequency zones (southern desert) use slow, ample movements. The underground deaf village uses a radically different 3-finger high-resolution dialect. Dialect is not cosmetic -- it affects mutual intelligibility.

**Optimization target:** Two citizens from different zones experience Contact friction (reduced comprehension speed, occasional misreads) that diminishes with sustained interaction. Dialect convergence is a measurable signal of relationship depth.

---

## O4: Contact as Relationship Measure

The richness of Contact between two citizens IS the measurement of their relationship. Pair-specific vocabulary size, gesture complexity, zone access (intimate zones like the nape vs collective zones like the shoulder), response latency -- all of these encode relational state more accurately than any explicit relationship score.

**Optimization target:** The graph weight between two citizen nodes correlates directly with Contact frequency, vocabulary size, and zone diversity. Contact IS the relationship -- no separate "relationship score" needed.

---

## O5: Linguistic Death Through Citizen Loss

When a citizen dies or departs, their idiolecte disappears. Every unique gesture they contributed to every pair-specific vocabulary is lost. The remaining citizens become linguistically poorer -- their expressive palette shrinks. This is not metadata cleanup. It is the central mechanic: the community's communicative capacity degrades with every departure.

**Optimization target:** Citizen departure triggers measurable vocabulary loss across all surviving partners. The loss should be observable, quantifiable, and irreversible.

---

## Tradeoffs

| Favored | Over | Reasoning |
|---------|------|-----------|
| Emergence | Pre-configuration | Idiolectes must form through interaction, not be assigned |
| Fidelity to source | Simplification | The 5-mode, zone-based, degradation-aware system must be preserved whole |
| Linguistic loss | Data preservation | Dead citizens' vocabulary must actually disappear, not be archived |
| Proximity constraint | Convenience | Contact requires adjacency -- no remote communication |
| Richness | Performance | A Contact gesture carries 6+ dimensions of data; do not flatten |
